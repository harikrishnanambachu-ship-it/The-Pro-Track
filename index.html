The Problem We Solve

Three compounding disadvantages every graduate faces.

Despite strong academic performance, most students aged 19–24 hit these walls the moment they enter the job market.

PROBLEM 01

Digital Invisibility

Most graduates have no LinkedIn presence or a hollow profile that says nothing beyond their college name. Recruiters scroll past. Opportunities never arrive. The internet can't find you if you don't exist on it.

PROBLEM 02

The Skills Gap

Degrees certify knowledge from 4 years ago. The market wants applied skills in AI, data, marketing, and product — learnable for free online but rarely taught in class. Certificates aren't proof; projects are.

PROBLEM 03

No Professional Network

Most students only know classmates and professors. First jobs come from networks. Without one, talented students lose to less-capable but better-connected peers who got the call before the job was posted.

PROBLEM 04

Content vs. Career Confusion

Students either avoid social media professionally or chase follower counts and viral posts. Neither leads to a job. LinkedIn is a career tool — used with intention, it's the fastest path from classroom to offer.

The Founders

Built by people who lived
the problem firsthand.

The ProTrack was created by two professionals who watched talented peers lose opportunities — not because of ability, but because of visibility. They built the system they wish had existed when they were students.

The problem wasn't talent. It was invisibility.

Kerala produces some of India's most academically capable graduates. Brilliant students — engineers, commerce graduates, science majors — finishing college every year with strong marks and weak networks.

They watched it happen repeatedly: talented peers who couldn't get interviews not because they lacked ability, but because the right people had never heard of them. LinkedIn profiles that said nothing. No professional presence, no proof of skill, no network outside the campus gate.

Meanwhile, less-capable students from metro cities — with better connections and a more visible online presence — walked into opportunities those Kerala graduates deserved. The playing field wasn't level. It never has been. But it can be leveled.

That's the insight that became The ProTrack: social media has shifted from a place to consume content to a powerful engine for career acceleration. LinkedIn, used with intention, is the fastest path from classroom to career — regardless of where you grew up.

FAQ

Common questions answered.

Do I need a laptop to participate?
No. The entire program is designed to run on a smartphone and a free internet connection. No paid tools, no laptop, no subscriptions required.
I have no LinkedIn profile. Can I still join?
Yes — in fact, that's the ideal starting point. Month 1 is entirely about building your profile from zero. You'll have a complete, professional profile by the end of Week 4.
What stream do I need to be from?
Any stream — engineering, commerce, arts, science, or management. The program is not technical. It builds professional skills that apply across all career paths.
How much time does it actually take per week?
3–5 hours per week outside college. This includes your weekly milestone, LinkedIn posts, skill learning, and group check-ins. It's designed to fit around your college schedule.
What happens if I miss a week?
Life happens. The group is understanding. But consistent attendance is what makes the program work — for you and for the batch. Accountability is the product.
Is there a guarantee I'll get a job?
No guarantee — and anyone who offers one is lying. What we guarantee is a system, a community, and 10 months of structured effort. Students who follow through get results. That's the only honest promise.
Will this work for someone outside Kerala?
Absolutely. The program was originally designed for Kerala students, but the system works for any college student in India. LinkedIn is a national — and global — platform.
What skill should I learn in Month 4?
That's your choice — and Month 4 starts by helping you make it. The program provides a market mapping framework to choose the skill that fits your career goal, then a free 8-week learning plan to master it.
