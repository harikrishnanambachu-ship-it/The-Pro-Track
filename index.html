About ProTrack

A degree no longer guarantees a career.
Employability is a skill — and it can be taught.

The ProTrack exists because of a simple, uncomfortable observation: a degree no longer guarantees a career. Employers consistently report that fresh graduates are technically qualified but professionally unprepared.

We believe employability is a skill set, not a personality trait — it can be taught, practiced, and proven, just like any technical subject. Real proof beats certificates. Small, consistent effort beats occasional intensity.

Real proof beats certificates. A student who has shipped a real project is more employable than one who has only attended a lecture about it.

Small, consistent effort beats intensity. Fifteen minutes a day, sustained over months, builds more durable capability than a one-day bootcamp.

These skills are domain-agnostic. Whether a student becomes an engineer, analyst, marketer, or manager — the same five capabilities determine success.
